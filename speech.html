Politics and evolution

 

The document on the left is a fragment from King's Sejong Hangul Proclamation, which was a reform introduced in 15th century in Korea aiming at simplifying the Chinese character alphabet into Korean character alphabet in order to increase literacy. This document also is a reminder of a struggle for linguistic identity through gradual secession of foreign linguistic influences. Korean language and the debate around it's identity has a fascinating history. After the division of Korea in 1945 the spoken language diverged in the North and South and linguists currently refer to the two as Northern and Southern dialects. The story of two Koreas is parallel to the linguistic evolution that took place after the Yugoslavian breakdown between 1990 and 1992, where each newly formed state was seeking its own form of expression by separating itself culturally and linguistically even further. In case of North Korea the linguistic evolution process was fostered by series of policy reforms regulating linguistic identity such as "Number of Issues on the Development of the Korean language" (조선어를 발전시키기 위한 몇 가지 문제) or "In Rightly Advancing the National Characteristics of the Korean language" (조선어의 민족적 특성을 옳게 살려 나갈 데 대하여). The most interesting divergences between the two major dialects were the vocabulary adjustments such as in Northern dialect people referr to events from 1950s as the War of the liberation of the motherland (조국해방전쟁) and in the south it is called the Korean War (한국 전쟁 ).

 

Spoken language evolution

The historians and linguists studying the evolution of spoken languages developed various models for evolution and the most commonly accepted one is the branching model of diverse language families aiming at tracing languages back to the proto-language (the hypothetical common ancestor). The video will give you a visual introduction to the model.

The interactive time series and spacial analysis map below was created by a research team at Publication Connection organization in Washington in 2016. It's a source that gives us an enormous insight into how spoken languages evolve as a result of lifestyle and living conditions changes.  Separation of populations from each other leads to further divergence of spoken languages. Also an interesting trends tend to appear when we select the Overlays tab and click on the Urbanization map. If one looks closely at the Urbanization changes over time one can notice that the natural languages overlap with some of the urbanization trends. I have included pictures for comparison of the main linguistic groups in India on the left and the Urbanization Map screen shot on the right that illustrates this overlap between language and urbanization.  Furthermore, by studying urbanization one can also notice that geography plays and important role in shaping not only where people decide to settle, but also how natural barriers create boarders which foster further linguistic divergence. In addition to geographical barriers linguistic evolution is also a statement of power and a tool of suppression of other cultures. At first glance solely a form of communication can become a weapon and a fostering factor for urbanization. The comparative technique of geopolitics analysis alongside with syntax analysis is the exact method that language historians use to trace back interdependencies between languages and create linguistic families maps.
